About

Hi, I am Taranveer Singh Gill. I am 24 years old and was born in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. I completed my schooling from Delhi Public School, Gandhidham. Growing up, my father ran an auto parts shop, which gave me a lot of exposure to mechanical things and cars from a young age. That’s what ignited my desire to pursue Mechanical Engineering, and I went on to do my bachelor's from Vellore Institute of Technology.

In the four years of my bachelor’s, I didn’t confine myself just to studies. I actively participated in extracurricular activities and represented my college in an international competition called Baja SAE held in the USA, where our team ranked 4th worldwide. That remains one of my best memories from college. I also spent many great evenings on campus playing badminton almost every day and became quite a pro at it later on.

But one day, while working on a project with students from multiple departments, we combined our knowledge and designed a drone to clean algae from water bodies. The drone was built using mechanical design software, and OpenCV was used for image processing to detect algae. That experience opened a whole new world to me, making me curious to learn more about this field of technology and data.

Later, I got a job as a Data Validation Engineer at Numeros Motors, where I worked for around a year. My role involved managing and analyzing large datasets from testing and validation processes to ensure the systems met specifications. I also implemented data visualization skills by creating clear and informative charts and reports, effectively communicating results to stakeholders and cross-functional teams.

After gaining some experience, I decided to pursue my Master’s in Data Science from the University at Buffalo (SUNY), USA which was honestly one of the best decisions of my career. I got to learn so many concepts in depth that are essential to becoming a proficient data scientist. Unlike my bachelor’s, I didn’t play any sports here because I realized that freedom comes with responsibility. I worked on campus to manage my expenses and I’m thankful to the university for that opportunity.

I truly believe I’ve learned a lot since I came to the USA. But now, I’m eager to put all my skills and knowledge to real-world use and work for a company that gives me the opportunity to improve and prove myself.
